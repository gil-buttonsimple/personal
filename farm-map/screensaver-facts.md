## Place
- Chester, the county seat, ~5 mi NW; Chester County founded 1785, named for Chester,
  Pennsylvania. (SC Encyclopedia: scencyclopedia.org/sce/entries/chester/)
- Piedmont uplands generally a few hundred to ~700 ft; town of Chester at 535 ft.
  (Wikipedia: Chester, South Carolina)
- Charlotte & South Carolina Railroad through Chester: chartered 1846, opened 1852,
  Charlotte NC to Columbia SC. (Wikipedia: Charlotte and South Carolina Railroad)

## Native peoples
- Catawba — "Ye Iswa," people of the river — historically held the Carolina Piedmont
  centered on the Catawba River valley. (SC Encyclopedia: .../catawbas/)
- Federally restored via the Catawba Land Claims Settlement Act, Oct 27 1993; the Nation's
  reservation is in York County, east of Rock Hill. (Wikipedia: Catawba people)

## Revolutionary War (SC backcountry, near here)
- Battle of Fishing Creek (Catawba Ford), Aug 18 1780: Tarleton routed Thomas Sumter's
  militia where Fishing Creek meets the Catawba, in present-day Chester County (SE toward
  Great Falls). (SC Encyclopedia: .../fishing-creek-battle-of/; American Battlefield Trust)
- Huck's Defeat (Williamson's Plantation), Jul 12 1780, at Brattonsville just north in York
  County — a turning point in the SC Revolution. (American Battlefield Trust)
- Thomas Sumter, "the Carolina Gamecock"; Cornwallis called him his "greatest plague."
  (SC Encyclopedia: .../sumter-thomas/)

## Civil War era (1865)
- The Confederate "treasure train" and Varina Davis's family party passed through Chester
  in April 1865; the rail line ended at Chester's depot. (NOTE: it was Mrs. Davis's party,
  not President Davis, who went through York County.) (Roots & Recall, Chester depot)

## Soil & agriculture — the "no soil" story
- The Southern Piedmont lost on average ~7 inches of topsoil to a century of cotton; in
  many places all of it. One of the most severely eroded farming regions in the US.
  (New Georgia Encyclopedia / Trimble; Soil & Water Conservation Society)
- Worn-out cotton fields reforested naturally in loblolly "old-field pine."
  (USDA Forest Service, SRS)
- The red Piedmont clay is the Cecil series, an iron-rich Ultisol; erosion stripped the
  topsoil and exposed the red clay subsoil. (USDA OSD; Wikipedia: Cecil soil)
  (NOTE: Cecil is NC's state soil; SC's is Lynchburg. Cecil is still the right red-clay story here.)

## Forest
- Oak-hickory hardwoods dominate the SC Piedmont (white oak, post oak, southern red oak,
  mockernut hickory); with loblolly, shortleaf and Virginia pine. (SC DNR SWAP; USFS LANDFIRE)
- Common companions: sweetgum, red maple, tulip poplar, flowering dogwood.

## Climate
- Average annual precipitation 45.9 in; average annual temperature 60.4 F (1991-2020 NOAA
  normals, Chester station). (SC State Climatology Office, Chester county data)
- Humid subtropical; growing season roughly April to early November.

## Hydrology
- On the divide between the Catawba (east) and Broad (west) drainages; local streams Sandy
  River and Fishing Creek. (SC DNR Major Basins)
- Catawba River near Rock Hill (USGS 02146000): annual mean discharge has ranged ~1,250 to
  ~10,260 cfs (dam-regulated). (USGS waterdata 02146000)

## Wildlife
- White-tailed deer, wild turkey, bobcat, beaver are all Piedmont/Sumter NF residents.
  (US Forest Service) The wood duck is SC's state duck (2009). (SC DNR)
- Farm-pond fish: largemouth bass, bluegill/bream, catfish (range-typical).
