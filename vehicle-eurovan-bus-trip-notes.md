# Bus Trip Notes -- 1999 VW Eurovan

## Electrical & Wiring

- **Solenoid relay (ignition)** -- failed. Considering replacing the key ignition with a switch instead of a new relay. Need to investigate whether this is safe/sound.
- **Mystery wires under passenger dash** -- two wires (black and green) coming out from under the passenger side dashboard. Initially thought they went to the Winnebago amp install, but when touched to something it sounded like the door locks triggered. Need to trace before removing.
- **Wiring removal (big category)** -- lots of old cabling to remove. Goal: remove as much as possible without disabling the electric locks. Treat the mystery wires above as a special case.
- **VW interior lighting** -- removing most of the remaining factory VW lighting. Already removed most. Still deciding on the one over the sliding door.

## HVAC / Climate

- **AC interior (blower) fan** -- started making a loud clicking noise. Suspect something fell into the blower box during relay work, or motor bearing failing. Procedure to access: glove box out, disconnect resistor harness, disengage locking tab, rotate motor clockwise, pull toward center of dash. Inspect for debris or rough bearing.
- **Rear bug screen** -- need a mosquito barrier for the back hatch on hot days. Doesn't need to be a door, can be permanent or snap-on. Screen door on sliding door is already done.

## Engine / Drivetrain

- **Cylinder 6 misfire** -- OBD2 showing misfires, mainly under load (accelerating uphill in top gear). Diagnostic order: swap coil/wire/plug from cyl 6 to another cylinder and see if it follows.

## Tires

- **Right front tire** -- was at 25 psi, refilled to 45 to match the others. Vehicle was drifting right for ~24 hrs before noticed.
- **Valve stems** -- rear stems are longer but still need to be long-stem style for easier access. Front requires nearly removing hubcaps to fill. Get proper long valve stems installed.
- **Rotation schedule** -- start rotating on a real schedule instead of slipping until something goes wrong.
